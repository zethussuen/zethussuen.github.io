Origin Story

Described by the VP of Content Planning & Analysis as the "Operating System of Netflix Studios", Origin Story tracks production details of Netflix Original projects from pitch to play.

Today, Origin Story tracks over 1,000 productions, cast & crew information, shooting locations, post production specs, and much more.

Orion

Netflix's content budget weighs in at $7B (and growing). To ensure each story gets the maximum exposure and playtime it deserves, we set out to create a programming tool that helps us find the perfect day to launch.

Orion is used by hundreds of studio employees to track, propose, and manage upcoming launches.

Memento

When you're filming in hundreds of locations across the world, you're supporting a crew of thousands of vendors. Memento is Netflix's premiere Accounts Payable tool used to onboard suppliers.

Studio Design System

How do you manage and streamline design across multiple engineering teams while maintining agility? By establishing a living design system, we are able to reduce repetitive UI choices and focus on world-class experiences.

Netflix's Studio Design System (SDS) is a collaborative effort between design and engineering to create a pattern library of components that serve as the building blocks to all things Netflix Studio.

Doug

Originally a Netflix Hack Day project, Doug was conceived to remove the need of a desktop device to access the seemingly endless amount of data stored by Netflix Studios. By using machine learning and natural-language processing algorithms, we put the power of Netflix's data one voice command away for studio execs on the go.

Fetch

Before the good old days of Invision's Craft, getting actual data into your static mocks was hard. I built an electron app and Sketch plugin that connects to any local databases and allows a user to fill in content based on layer names and table columns.

As Sketch changed its and Craft became more popular, I decided to halt development of Fetch in 2016. In other words, I stopped trying to make Fetch happen.

Livefyre Bootstrap (LFBS)

Before LFBS, all of Livefyre's front-end was managed on an app-by-app basis. By maintaining a common library for all Livefyre properties, we were able to ship more consistent experiences on faster timelines.

We also maintained a library of bespoke icons unique to Livefyre’s platform called “Fycons”. Using gulp and SketchTool CLI, we generated iconfonts directly from the Sketchfile.
